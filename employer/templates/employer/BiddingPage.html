We're now ready to start evaluating the applicants.

The first 10 application portfolios you see will show how the applicant in each decision answered a question in which they indicated whether they thought that their performance on the test, relative to others, was terrible, not good, neutral, good, very good, or exceptional. You will use that information to make a valuation for each applicant.


To help you with your valuations, keep in mind the following:

  • The average number of evaluation questions correctly answered by a job applicant is {{ Constants.mean_performance }} out of 10.
  • The valuation you choose for each applicant should be the maximum amount you would be willing to exchange in return for 10 cents times the number of job application questions correctly answered by the applicant.

The next 10 application portfolios you see will show how the applicant in each decision indicated their level of agreement on a scale from 0 (entirely disagree) to 100 (entirely agree) with the following statements:

  • I performed well on the test I took in part 1.
  • Usually I am the best at what I do.
  • I conduct all tasks assigned to me with the needed attention.

You will use that information to make a valuation for each applicant.


Again, keep in mind the following:

  • The average number of evaluation questions correctly answered by a job applicant is {{ Constants.mean_performance }} out of 10.
  • The valuation you choose for each applicant should be the maximum amount you would be willing to exchange in return for 10 cents times the number of job application questions correctly answered by the applicant.

The next 10 profiles you see will each have one of the following statements, which the applicant in that decision chose to include:

  • "Usually I am the best at what I do."
  • "I conduct all tasks assigned to me with the needed attention."
  • I prefer not to include either of these statements in my application.

You will use that information to make a valuation for each applicant.


Again, keep in mind the following:

  • The average number of evaluation questions correctly answered by a job applicant is {{ Constants.mean_performance }} out of 10.
  • The valuation you choose for each applicant should be the maximum amount you would be willing to exchange in return for 10 cents times the number of job application questions correctly answered by the applicant.
