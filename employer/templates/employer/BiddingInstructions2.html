In each question you pick either Option A (10 cents for each job performance questions that the applicant answered correctly) or Option B (the fixed payment). After you answer all 100 questions, one row of the table above will be randomly picked and you will be paid for the option you chose on that one question. Each question is equally likely to be chosen for payment. It's in your interest to be honest on each question, because otherwise you may end up with the option you like less.

Most people will choose Option A in at least the first few rows, but at some point switch to choosing Option B. So, to save time, we'll just ask you at which cent value you want to switch, and we'll then automatically "fill out" your answers to all 100 rows based on your switching point (choosing Option A for all rows before your switching point, and Option B for all rows at or after your switching point).
